📊 Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio
Think of P/E like a price tag. If a stock has a P/E of 20, you're paying $20 for every $1 of annual profit. A lower P/E can mean cheaper — but always compare within the same industry. A P/E of 30 is normal in tech.
